Pour-over clarity at home.

A calm method for bloom, pulse, and finish when you want cafe texture without leaving the kitchen.

The best pour-over starts before water touches coffee. Warm the brewer, rinse the paper, and let the cup hold heat while you grind. Small details make the brew feel more generous.

For Kissa Noir beans, we like a medium-fine grind, 92°C water, and a 1:15.5 ratio. The goal is not theatrical pouring. The goal is even contact and a cup that opens slowly as it cools.

Coffee becomes memorable when extraction feels quiet, balanced, and intentional.

Bloom with patience

Use twice the coffee weight in water for the bloom. Wait 35 to 45 seconds, then watch for the bed to settle. A good bloom releases aroma without forcing movement.

Pulse, then listen

Pour in two or three gentle pulses. Keep the stream low, circle inward, and avoid washing the paper walls. The finished bed should be mostly flat with a soft walnut fragrance.
